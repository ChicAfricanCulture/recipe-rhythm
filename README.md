Heritage Harvest: An Interactive Journey Through African Food & Foodways


Gameplay & Learning Objectives

· Regional Exploration: Navigate through different African regions (West, East, North, Southern Africa) to discover staple ingredients.
· Ingredient Spotting: Identify and gather indigenous crops like Fonio, Teff, Okra, and Yams.
· Cooking Mechanics: Simulate traditional cooking methods to prepare iconic dishes (e.g., Jollof Rice, Injera with Doro Wat, Brik).
· Cultural Context: Unlock "Foodway Facts" that explain the rituals, history, and social significance of the meals.

Overview

African Foodways: An Interactive Exhibit is a digital interactive experience designed to document and celebrate the culinary heritage of the African continent. This project serves as a bridge between academic research and public engagement, using the medium of gaming to tell the stories that cookbooks alone cannot convey.

The Mission

Food is memory. Food is identity. This interactive portfolio explores:

· Indigenous Ingredients: The story of crops native to Africa and their global journey.
· Methods & Technology: Traditional tools like the mortar and pestle, and cooking techniques passed down through generations.
· Diaspora & Influence: How African foodways traveled and transformed across the Atlantic, shaping the cuisines of the Americas.

Academic Sources

The content of this game is fact-checked and researched using works by culinary historians such as Jessica B. Harris and Michael W. Twitty, ensuring an authentic and respectful representation of the cultures depicted.
